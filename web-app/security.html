Security Precautions

Revision Date: September 19th, 2020

  • Our domain is protected with CloudFlare, which hides the location of our internal servers, provides DDOS protection, and enforces our TLS rules.
    • Essentially this means that because your data is sent to CloudFlare before it is sent to us, a bad actor would need to penetrate a global company known for its exceptional security. CloudFlare CANNOT read any of your information.
  • Traffic from every part of our system (including internal monitoring services and Kubernetes services) are encrypted with TLS v1.3 connectionsend-to-end. For web services (externally facing), traffic is encrypted from your computer to cloudflare, and from cloudflare to our NGINX Ingress.
    • This means that there are no ways for an attacker to intercept unencrypted traffic before it reaches our servers.
  • Qualys SSL Labs, an external security audit platform, rated our encryption protocols an “A”, which means that it is extremely secure against any known exploits, such as POODLE, Heartbleed, SSL 3.0, and insecure cipher suites.
    • This means that third-party audits have not found any issues with our encryption standard.
    • Check out our rating here: https://www.ssllabs.com/ssltest/analyze.html?d=marintracingapp.org&latest 
  • Our application runs on Google Server Infrastructure (specifically Google Kubernetes Engine on GCP). Google implements technical and physical safeguards to protect customer data from misuse. Access privileges when we are developing the platform are given with the Principle of Least Privilege (people are only given privileges for services that they need).
    • This means that you can rest assured that your data is stored in secure data centers.
  • For platforms that support it, all third party services are configured to use two factor authentication. All internal passwords are above 32 characters long, and consist of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Through Auth0, which provides our authentication infrastructure, we use an RBAC (role-based access control) model to control who has access to our externally and internally facing services. 
    • For example, we only permit users who have a specified role to access the admin dashboard.
    • All public facing URLs are protected by this RBAC system— either through a Bearer Authorization Header, or through a Cookie issued through an OIDC Proxy.
  • We limit access privileges to prevent users from tampering with or seeing information they aren’t supposed to
    • When you login to the community edition, we limit your access privileges with a JSON Web Token (JWT), so you can only report symptoms, contacts, and tests for yourself. 
    • The same is true for the admin platform— we only allow admins to see the dashboard for schools they have been granted explicit access to manage
    • JSON Web Tokens are industry-standard untamperable identifiers that cannot be changed. They also have an expiration date, to prevent usage if it gets into the wrong hands.
  • All services that do not need to be exposed cannot be accessed externally. They only live within the Kubernetes cluster. 
    • For example, our vault, database, and monitoring services are not accessible from the internet
  • All passwords, school configuration modules, and API Keys are stored in a high availability deployment of HashiCorp Vault.
    • HashiCorp Vault is a widely-used platform for storing secure data, and it is used by many Fortune 500 companies. This means that you can be confident that our secure information is protected.
  • We have daily backups of our Neo4J Database PVC to prevent data loss in the case of an error/outage
    • Essentially this means that all of your data is backed up, in the case of an error, exploit, or outage.
